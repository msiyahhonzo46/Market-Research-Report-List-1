<p><p>Precision casting, also known as investment casting, is a manufacturing process that enables the production of complex metal parts with high precision and excellent surface finishes. This method involves creating a wax pattern that is coated with a heat-resistant ceramic material. Once the ceramic hardens, the wax is melted away, leaving a cavity that is then filled with molten metal. Precision casting is widely used across various industries, including aerospace, automotive, and medical, due to its ability to produce intricate designs and reduce material waste.</p><p>The Precision Casting Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.4% during the forecast period. <p><p>The precision casting market encompasses various methods, including Silica Sol Investment Casting and Water Glass Investment Casting, among others. Silica Sol Investment Casting utilizes a soluble silica binder for high precision and surface finish, making it ideal for complex geometries. In contrast, Water Glass Investment Casting employs sodium silicate as a binder, offering cost-efficiency for larger parts. Other methods in the market may include ceramic shell casting or lost wax techniques, catering to diverse industrial applications such as aerospace, automotive, and medical sectors.</p></p>
