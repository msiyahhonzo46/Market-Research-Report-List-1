<p><p>Multiphoton microscopy encompasses various techniques for imaging biological samples using multiple photons for excitation. Two-photon excitation utilizes two photons of lower energy to excite fluorophores, enabling deeper tissue imaging with reduced photodamage. Three-photon excitation employs three photons, allowing for even deeper penetration and improved resolution, ideal for thick samples. Other methods include advanced techniques like higher-order multiphoton excitation and nonlinear optical imaging, which expand imaging capabilities across diverse applications in neuroscience, developmental biology, and materials science.</p></p>
