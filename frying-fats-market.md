<p><p>The frying fats market is primarily divided into two categories: vegetable fats and animal fats. Vegetable fats, derived from plant sources like oils (e.g., sunflower, canola, and palm), are favored for their health benefits and versatility in cooking. They have high smoke points, making them ideal for frying. In contrast, animal fats, such as lard and tallow, are known for their rich flavor and high melting points. Each type serves distinct culinary applications and caters to different consumer preferences and dietary considerations.</p></p>
